Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do I obtain access to this website for my high school students?
    • Student access to the site is provided through their teacher. Instructor access is provided upon textbook adoption. A 6" x 9" folded access card should be included with a school's textbook shipment. The access card contains registration instructions and access codes for teachers and students. A single set of website access codes can be used by two teachers and up to 50 students for one year. During registration, instructors and students create a personal login name and password, which they then use to log into the site each time they visit. Individual login-password information is required for students to view the results of their own work.

      If for some reason the access card is not included with the first shipment of texts, or an instructor inherits a set of texts, new access codes can be requested by emailing phwebaccess@pearsoned.com. In subsequent out-years of the adoption, registered teachers will be sent an email in the summer with information for setting up website access for a new set of students.

  • What happened to The Biology Place website?
    • It is still available. Go to www.biology.com. This open-access website provides students and teachers with a comprehensive online glossary, BioCoach activities to help students visualize and apply biology concepts, and LabBench with pre- and post-lab reviews that correspond to the prescribed AP biology labs.

  • What is "MasteringBiology" and can I use it with high school students?
    • MasteringBiology is an optional, for purchase-only, online homework and tutoring system. Students work through in-depth tutorials, receiving hints, feedback, and guidance in real time. Teachers can assign quizzes and homework to students and then view student progress through the gradebook function. Student records can be exported to another course management system or to commercial spreadsheets. MasteringBiology may be purchased in class registration kits containing either 10 or 25 access codes. One access code is for 1 student for 12 months beginning at the time of registration. For more information, speak to your sales representative or visit www.masteringbio.com.

  • What are the BioFlix animations and how can I use them?
    • BioFlix animations are movie-quality, 3-D animations and tutorials that can be assigned as homework or used to invigorate classroom lectures. These interactive tutorials help students learn the most challenging biological topics with 3-D animations plus labeled slideshows, basic tutorials, study sheets, and quizzes. They are available on this website—look for the BIoFlix link in the left-hand navigation column.

  • Where are the answers to the LabBench quizzes?
    • They are on the instructor version of this website. If you've logged on as a teacher, click on "For Instructors" in the left-hand navigation bar and find them under the heading "Lab Media" in this new window. The instructor's assets are not located on the CD and are online only.

  • Where are the Biology Labs On-Line?
    • Biology Labs On-Line is an optional, for-purchase only product. These twelve optional labs allow students to perform potentially dangerous, lengthy, or expensive experiments in a safe electronic environment. Additional product information, as well as details on how to purchase, is available at http://biologylab.awlonline.com

  • Will I need to do anything to my computer to use the website or CD-ROM?
    • The Site Requirements page indicates what you need to optimize your computer for use of the website or CD-ROM: system requirements, web browsers that are supported, and required plug-ins.

  • Do I need an internet connection to use the CD-ROM?
    • The CD-ROM does not require an internet connection for the Chapter Guide, Activities and Activities Quiz, Practice Test, Investigations, Videos, GraphIt! and LabBench. An internet connection is required for the E-Book, Self Quiz, BioFlix, MP3 Tutors, Discovery Videos, Biology Labs On-Line, Word Roots, Key Terms, Glossary, Flashcards, Art, Cumulative Test, Campbell Interviews, and accessing external sites like the Biological Inquiry Workbook Website.

  • Can I access the website from my home and school computers?
    • Yes. Your subscription includes access through your Login Name and Password from any computer with an internet connection—at home, school, work, or the library. Just go to www.campbellbiology.com, click on your book cover, and enter your Login Name and Password. You may only use your Login Name and Password from one computer at a time, however.

  • What if I forget my website Login Name and Password?
    • Contact Technical Support and indicate that you have forgotten your Login Name and Password.

  • How do I contact Technical Support?
    • Contact the Technical Support team for the CD-ROM and/or website via the internet.

  • How do I print materials from the CD-ROM and website?
    • Most areas within the CD-ROM and website work within frames. To print from a framed page, you must move your cursor to the part of the page you wish to print and select it. Then send the page to the printer. If you want to print different frames on a given page, you will need to select each section and print them individually.

  • How do I submit my quiz answers?
    • When using the CD-ROM to complete Practice Tests, Activities and Investigations Quizzes, and GraphIt! Quizzes, click the "Submit Answers for Grading" button located at the end of each quiz to see your results. These results can be emailed to your instructor from the results screen by filling in the appropriate information and clicking the "E-Mail Results" button.
      When using the live website, Self Quizzes, Activities Quizzes, Practice Tests, Investigations Quizzes, and quizzes for GraphIt!, BioFlix, MP3 Tutors, and Discovery Videos are automatically submitted to your personal Results Reporter and to your Instructor's online gradebook (if you are enrolled in your instructor's course) by clicking on the "Submit Answers for Grading" button located at the end of each quiz.

  • Does it matter if I am using a PC or a Macintosh?
    • No. The website and CD-ROM can be used from a PC or a Macintosh as long as the computer meets the minimum site requirements.

  • I can't get past the website login page when I enter my Login name and Password. What is wrong?
    • The server is unable to set the necessary cookies to allow you to navigate the website. You will need to check a few things:
      1. that the version of browser you're using is supported (please see the Site Requirements page);
      2. that the "cookie" setting on your browser (usually found under the "Options" or "Preferences" menu) is set to accept cookies; and
      3. that you don't have any programs installed which automatically refuse all cookies, such as "Cookie Cutter" or "WebFree."
      If all these things check out and you are still unable to log in to the website, please contact Technical Support.

  • Why can't I eject the CD-ROM from my Macintosh hard drive?
    • To eject the CD-ROM from a Macintosh you need to drag the CD-ROM icon to the Trash bin. If you get a message that your CD-ROM will not eject from your Macintosh hard drive then restart your computer and load the CD-ROM in a different browser.

  • I don't see the graphics on some screens. In the place where I expect something to appear I only see a plug-in icon. What should I do?
    • One or more plug-ins are not up to date or are not installed correctly for your browser. You will need to update or install the plug-ins by visiting the specific website for each plug-in.

  • How do I turn off the narration or adjust the volume?
    • Adjust the volume using your computer's sound control or speaker knobs. To turn off the sound completely, turn the volume down as far as possible or, if you have the option, select "Mute."

  • I can't see the bottom of the graphics in the activities. What can I do?
    • Your screen is either too small or at too low a resolution. Try the following: move the browser window to the top of your screen and make it bigger; increase your screen resolution (it must be at least 1024x768); hide your browser's tool bars; or, try refreshing/reloading the screen.

  • My browser gives me a message that it is running low on memory. What should I do?
    • Try quitting any other applications you have open. There may be unnecessary programs running in the background that you can also quit.

  • I have installed QuickTime and now I get a message asking if I want to order QuickTime Pro. Do I have to buy QuickTime Pro to run the CD-ROM or website?
    • No. The basic version of QuickTime does everything that you need to run the CD-ROM and website.

  • I installed QuickTime on my PC and I can't view any animations. What should I do?
    • The QuickTime installer for PCs has been known to make QuickTime the preferred player for Flash content (animations) even though it cannot always play this content. To change your QuickTime preferences, exit your browser, click on the start menu, click on settings, then click on Control Panel. Then select QuickTime and choose Browser plug-in from the QuickTime drop down box. Click on the MIME settings button, choose miscellaneous check box to remove the check mark. Close the QuickTime control panel window and restart your browser.

  • When I try to register at the website I get a message that I need an access code. Where do I get an access code?
    • Your access code is in your textbook. To access the website, follow the instructions on the insert just inside the front cover.

  • My access code doesn't work. Now what?
    • If you bought a used book, your access code may have been used by a previous owner. To obtain a new access code, purchase a website subscription at your bookstore or online. To purchase online, go to www.campbellbiology.com and click on your book cover. Click "Buy Now" to purchase a website subscription. If you purchased a new book and your access code doesn't work, contact Technical Support.

  • Instructors: How do I track my students' quiz results in the online gradebook?
    • Once you have registered for the website, you may "Create a Class" and generate a Class ID your students will use to join your class. Once you've created this class and your students have joined it, their quiz results will appear in your online gradebook.

  • Students: How do I make sure my quiz results are recorded in my instructor's online gradebook?
    • Once you have registered for the website, you may "Join a Class" using a Class ID supplied by your instructor. Once you've joined, your quiz results will appear in your instructor's gradebook.
