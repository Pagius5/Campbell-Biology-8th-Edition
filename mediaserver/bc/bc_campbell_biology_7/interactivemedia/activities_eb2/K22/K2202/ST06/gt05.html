Now consider the last kind of undigested food molecule. Lipids remain in the form of large globules. These globules cannot be easily digested by the lipid hydrolases, called lipases, unless they are separated into smaller globules. Fortunately, a substance called bile is added to the small intestine to help separate or emulsify the lipid globules.
