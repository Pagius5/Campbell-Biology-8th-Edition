Food undergoes four stages of processing in your body. In the first stage, food enters the mouth and is chewed during the process of ingestion. Next, food polymers are broken apart into monomers in the process called digestion. In humans, this occurs mostly in the stomach and small intestine. Next, monomer nutrients and water are taken up by the cells of the small and large intestine in a process called absorption. Finally, undigested food passes out of the body through the anus in the process of elimination.
