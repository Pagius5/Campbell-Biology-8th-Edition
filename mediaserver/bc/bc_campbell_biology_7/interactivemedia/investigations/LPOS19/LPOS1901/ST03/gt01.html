The latest methods for producing genetically engineered organisms involve the creation of artificial genes, called expression systems, suited for specific purposes. For example, a researcher needing a protein expressed regularly in a root cell of a plant must find an enhancer, proximal control region and promoter combination permitting that type of expression.

In this investigation, you will practice producing an expression system from the gene parts provided. To do this, click on a DNA segment and drag it into a location on the outline of the DNA strand in the upper portion of the frame.

Look for the results of your selection and then proceed to the next step when you have succeeded in getting protein expression.
