For the control test group, you will use a proximal control region normally found in yeast that allows continuous binding of the RNA polymerase to the promoter. Therefore, an enhancer region will not be needed.

The reporter genes for this experiment will be the enzymes firefly luciferase and beta-D-glucuronidase. Firefly luciferase is tested by looking for a yellow glow when the substrates luciferin and ATP are added. The brighter the glow, the greater is the expression of firefly luciferase. Beta-D-glucuronidase is indicated by looking for a blue color to the yeast when 5-bromo-4-chloro-3-indolyl glucuronide substrate is added under special conditions. The darker the blue, the greater is the Beta-D-glucuronidase expression.

Before doing the experiment, first note the appearance of the yeast cells in the 6 petri plates. These cells are not expressing either firefly luciferase or beta-D-glucuronidase.

Build the expression system by clicking on a DNA segment and dragging it into the proper location on the outline of the DNA strand in the upper portion of the frame.
