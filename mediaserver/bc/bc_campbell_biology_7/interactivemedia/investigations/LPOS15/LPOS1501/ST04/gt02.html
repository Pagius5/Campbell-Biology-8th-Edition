In the first week, remove adults before new flies emerge. Then, separate males from females. Using ice, chemicals, or carbon dioxide, anaesthetize the specimens and note any variations. Some of your flies will be of the wild type (showing the normal phenotype) and others may show various mutations (genetic variations from the wild type). Common mutations you may see are ebony body, wingless, white eyes, or sepia eyes. Click on the Questions window and answer Question 1.
