Prokaryotes can be grouped into four categories according to how they obtain energy and carbon. There are photoautotrophs, chemoautotrophs, photoheterotrophs, and chemoheterotrophs.

In this activity, you will investigate prokaryote nutrition by selecting factors that will support the growth of the bacteria in the petri dishes shown at left.

1. Before performing the experiment, click on the Questions window and use what you learned from your readings to answer Question 1.

2. The bacterial growth chamber at left will allow you to test modes of prokaryote nutrition. One by one, choose a culture to grow by dragging each petri dish to the chamber.

3. Use the chamber buttons to select the minimum factors— an energy source and a carbon source— that the bacteria need to live.

4. Click on Check Answer to see whether you have selected the correct factors to support the bacteria's growth. Click Reset to start over.
