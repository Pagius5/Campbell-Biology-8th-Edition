Plants have a remarkable ability to replace lost organs— roots and shoots. Control of organ initiation involves hormones, and we exploit this fact commercially to grow plants from stem cuttings, blocks of tissue, or single cells.

You're working at a biotech company, and you've been asked to experiment on a block of tissue from a previously unstudied plant. Choose the two hormones that you think are most likely to induce organ formation— the development of roots or shoots. Drag two droppers to the empty dish. Then drag the tissue to the empty dish and watch the results. Good luck!
