TEST 2 – Modified Hypothesis: Some spores and/or mycelium arrive after manure is deposited and contribute to the population of Pilobolus on the manure. An experimental approach.

1. Randomly collect 40, 100g samples of manure that are as fresh as possible.
2. Randomly select 10 of the samples and set them aside. Plate out these samples and label as Treatment I.
3. Sterilize the other 30 samples in an autoclave. (This is an apparatus that kills organisms by the use of heat and pressure.)
4. Plate out 10 of the sterilized samples and immediately cover the dishes. This is Treatment II.
5. Plate out another 10 sterilized samples, and leave them exposed to the air in the pasture for one day before covering the dishes. This is Treatment III.
6. Plate out the final 10 sterilized samples and inoculate them with growing mycelium of Pilobolus. Cover them as soon as they are inoculated. This is Treatment IV.
