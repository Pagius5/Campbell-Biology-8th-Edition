Since the normal and disease alleles of the HD gene differ in the number of copies of the CAG repeat (glutamine, or "Q"), if we could detect that difference, we could identify people who have or will get the disease.

But how can we detect that difference? Enzymes called restriction enzymes cut DNA molecules at specific DNA sequences. For example, one enzyme called AluI (pronounced "a'-loo one") cuts the sequence AGCT wherever it occurs. AluI cuts this sequence between the G and the C, straight through both DNA strands. For example, in the sequence below, the enzyme would find the AGCT sequence and cut between the G and the C.
