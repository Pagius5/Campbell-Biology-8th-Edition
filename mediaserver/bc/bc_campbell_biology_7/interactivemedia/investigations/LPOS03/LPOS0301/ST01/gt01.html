This activity introduces some of the processes of science— making observations, asking questions, gathering information, posing hypotheses, testing hypotheses experimentally, and reaching conclusions— through a study of acid precipitation.

Acid precipitation (rain, snow, fog) has an especially high concentration of hydrogen ions. In other words, it is acidic. Resulting mainly from the burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and gas), acid precipitation is a major environmental problem because it can kill aquatic organisms, destroy forests, and damage buildings and monuments.
