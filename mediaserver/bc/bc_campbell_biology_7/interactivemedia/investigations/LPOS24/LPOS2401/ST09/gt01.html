There is a more complex, and apparently more common, origin of allopolyploid species. Let's again look at two parent species, W and X, with diploid numbers of 4 and 6.

In plant W, an error in meiosis creates a gamete with 2 sets of chromosomes instead of one. It combines with a gamete from X, producing a hybrid with 7 chromosomes. Meiosis cannot occur normally in this hybrid, but imagine that abnormal meiosis produces a gamete with all 7 chromosomes. This gamete joins a normal gamete from X containing three chromosomes, to produce a zygote with a total of 10 chromosomes. Let us call this new plant Q.
