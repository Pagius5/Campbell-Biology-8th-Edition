Pharmacodynamics is the study of how a drug affects the body, including possible side effects. The shape, electrical charge, and chemistry of a drug molecule are all factors that determine its pharmacodynamic properties. For example, two drug enantiomers — molecules that are mirror images — may have quite different effects simply because of their subtle difference in shape.
