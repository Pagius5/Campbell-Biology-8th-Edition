Pharmacokinetics is the study of how a drug is transported through the body. The electrical charge and functional groups of a drug molecule can determine its ability to be transported to the affected parts of the body before being removed. Removal of a drug by the body, or drug clearance, is primarily done by the liver, where enzymes break down the drug and diminish its effect. Drugs are also removed by the kidneys.
