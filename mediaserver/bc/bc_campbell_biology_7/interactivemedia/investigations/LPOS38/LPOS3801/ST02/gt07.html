A good library can save years of research, though it takes patience. If your college has electronic databases, you can search for articles on Pectis papposa, or desert seed germination. Recent articles may lead to older works that you only find with a trip to dusty bookshelves. But it's worth the trouble! You find good leads in Seed Germination in Desert Plants, by Y. Gutterman, published by Springer-Verlag in 1993. It's mostly about Israel, but it mentions Went's studies on our Pectis in America. From Gutterman's book you get ideas about how plants like Pectis know when to germinate, but there's nothing conclusive about Pectis itself. You must do your own experiments!
