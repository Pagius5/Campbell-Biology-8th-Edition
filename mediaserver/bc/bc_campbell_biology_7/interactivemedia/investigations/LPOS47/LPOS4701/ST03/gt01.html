Recall from the previous screen that the PMCs migrate to form a latitudinal ring lining the inner surface of the outer (ectodermal) layer of cells. The position of this ring varies from species to species, as shown here for species A and B. We will perform transplantation experiments in which we remove PMCs from one species (the "donor"), and inject them among the PMCs of another embryo (the "host"). The experiment will be performed at the mesenchyme blastula stage, when the PMCs have already entered the blastocoel, but have not yet migrated to form the PMC ring. We will observe development to note the position of the ring.
