The 8 equal-sized cells on the top are called mesomeres. They will give rise to the ectoderm, the cells which will become the outer covering of the embryo.

The 4 large macromeres in the bottom half give rise to some ectoderm, all of the endoderm (tissue which will form the entire digestive tract), and some of the mesoderm (which forms the tissues in between such as muscle cells).

The 4 small micromeres at the bottom will give rise to mesoderm, tissue that will make the embryonic skeleton.

These cells will continue to divide, eventually forming a hollow ball of cells called the blastula.
