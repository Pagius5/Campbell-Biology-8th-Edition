The primary mesenchyme cells will migrate around the blastocoel cavity, on the inner surface of the outer cells. Meanwhile, a dimple will form on the bottom (at the "vegetal plate") which will extend upwards as a tube. This will be the digestive tract. Its formation is called gastrulation, and the next stage is called the gastrula.

The primary mesenchyme cells will join together in a latitudinal ring around the inside of the outer layer of cells, somewhere below the equator. Within this ring, the first skeletal spicules will form.
