The fertilized egg is enclosed within a fertilization membrane. All the divisions of the cleavage stages will occur within this protective membrane. The first three cleavages give rise to 8 cells that appear identical, in 2 tiers of 4 cells each. The fourth division is distinctive: The upper cells divide longitudinally to form a tier of 8 equal cells, while the lower cells divide latitudinally, and unequally, to form 4 large macromeres and 4 small micromeres.
