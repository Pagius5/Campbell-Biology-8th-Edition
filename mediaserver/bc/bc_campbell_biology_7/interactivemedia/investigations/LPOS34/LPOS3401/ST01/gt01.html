There is widespread agreement that birds evolved from reptiles; in fact, they are technically reptiles. Birds have scales on their feet and an amniotic egg like modern reptiles. They also share many skeletal similarities.

The oldest known birdlike animal (known through fossils) is Archaeopteryx lithographia. This proto-bird lived about 145 million years ago during the Age of the Dinosaurs. Archaeopteryx shows many features that suggest it is a transitional form between dinosaur reptiles and modern birds. It has teeth like dinosaurs, clawed forelimbs, and a long tail containing vertebrae. Unlike dinosaurs, Archaeopteryx had feathers, and probably was a weak flier.
