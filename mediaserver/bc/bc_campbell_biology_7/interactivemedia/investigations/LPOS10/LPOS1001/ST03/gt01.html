The next step is to place the paper with the sample (leaf stain) on it in a graduated cylinder containing an organic solvent such as a mixture of ether and acetone. The solvent separates the pigments of the chloroplasts. Note that the tip of the filter paper touches the solvent, but the pigment line is above it. Click the Start button to observe plant pigments separating as the solvent migrates upward. Then click the Stop button before any pigments run off the paper strip.
