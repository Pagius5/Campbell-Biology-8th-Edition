Leaves are not attached to stems in a random way. There is a very specific pattern.

Observe the illustrations at left, showing the most common leaf attachment patterns and locate the important axillary buds. There are three main patterns:

- Alternate: has one leaf attached along a stem (with no other leaf at that site).
- Opposite: has two leaves at each site, the leaf arrangement is opposite.
- Whorled: has more than two leaves attached at a single site, the leaf arrangement is whorled.
