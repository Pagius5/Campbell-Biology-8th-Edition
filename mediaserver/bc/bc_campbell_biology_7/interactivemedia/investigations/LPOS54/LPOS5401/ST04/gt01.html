2. Calculate gross primary production. Remember that the bottle with 0% light is the dark bottle. It has 1 mg O2/l. The light bottle is the bottle for which you are trying to figure production. To calculate gross primary production, you subtract the % O2 in the dark bottle from the % O2 in the light bottle. Let's do the 10% light bottle as an example.

Gross primary production = 3 mg O2/l - 1 mg O2/l = 2 mg O2/1

3. Calculate net primary production. Again, the light bottle is the one you are doing the calculation for, and you are given the value for the initial bottle as 4 mg O2/l. To calculate net productivity you subtract the % O2 in the initial bottle from the % O2 in the light bottle. Let's do this for the 10% light bottle again:

Net primary production = 3 mg O2/l - 4 mg O2/l = -1 mg O2/l
