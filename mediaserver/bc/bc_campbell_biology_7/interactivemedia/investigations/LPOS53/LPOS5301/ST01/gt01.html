Ecologists use a diversity index (H) to represent the diversity and species richness of organisms in an environment. The higher the absolute value of H, the greater the biodiversity. Species richness is the number of different types of organisms in an environment. Species richness and the diversity index together provide a way to analyze the health of an ecosystem. The H formula used here is the Shannon-Weiner Index, which measures both species richness and evenness. The value of H' increases as richness and evenness increase. The formula is: H1 = - (Pi) (log Pi), where Pi = the number of individuals of species i ÷ the total number of individuals in the community.
