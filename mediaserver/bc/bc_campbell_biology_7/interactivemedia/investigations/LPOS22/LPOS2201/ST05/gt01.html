Pesticides can directly poison the leafhoppers or kill off predators or competitors. Various pesticides work in different ways to control insects. Mild pesticides irritate the insect's respiratory system and may scare them off or cause them to suffocate. Stronger pesticides interfere with the insect's metabolism by either affecting cellular respiration or altering nervous system function.
