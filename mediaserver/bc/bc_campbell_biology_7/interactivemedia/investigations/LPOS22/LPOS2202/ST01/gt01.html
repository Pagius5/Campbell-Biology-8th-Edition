The news is full of stories about new strains of deadly bacteria. At one time, these organisms were easily treated with commonly available antibiotics. Today many bacteria have become antibiotic resistant. Antibiotic-resistance traits can be transmitted from one bacterium to another through genetic exchange.

Antibiotic resistance can result from the regular use of antibiotics to treat common bacterial infections. Ultimately, this leads to the selection of bacterial populations that are genetically capable of counteracting the antibiotics. Improper use, such as taking too small a dose or not completing a treatment, can also encourage selection of resistant populations.
