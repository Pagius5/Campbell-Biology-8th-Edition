The test tube with no antibiotics has bacterial growth, as expected. Growth is also seen in the test tube with penicillin, indicating that this strain of bacteria is penicillin-resistant. However, the ampicillin prevented bacterial growth. Biologists regularly develop new antibiotics and alternative therapies to combat antibiotic-resistant bacteria. Bacteria can develop metabolisms that are not affected by the antibiotics. Others produce enzymes that degrade the antibiotics. Some can immediately reject antibiotics.
