How can the rate of cellular respiration be measured? When you study the equation for cellular respiration, you will see that there are at least three ways:

1. Measure the amount of glucose consumed.
2. Measure the amount of oxygen consumed.
3. Measure the amount of carbon dioxide produced.

In this experiment, we will measure the amount of oxygen consumed.

The illustration at left shows you the basic features of a respirometer. This instrument measures changes in gas volume related to the consumption of oxygen.

Cellular respiration occurs in the cells of both plants and animals. You can construct a respirometer by putting any small organism in a vial with a pipette attached.
