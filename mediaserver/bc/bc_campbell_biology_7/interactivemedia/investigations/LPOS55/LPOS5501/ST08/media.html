To: Director of Restoration Projects Department of Natural Resources State of Iowa
From: Prairies-R-Us
Re: Prairie Restoration Analysis
We are happy to report that we have completed our study of prairie restoration sites for the State of Iowa. Our findings suggest that for maximum efficiency, a restoration site should be located an established prairie. We found that when a restoration site was 10 km away from an established prairie, it was difficult for species such as to colonize successfully. An effective restoration site should also be fairly in size. In our study, small sites of 1 acre did not provide sufficient habitat to support larger animal species such as .  We have also examined how the number of species at a restoration site might change over time. The increase in the number of species at the site will decline over time as begin to limit the total number of species at the site. The final equilibrium number of species present will be the highest in a site that is an existing prairie. We look forward to working with you on future restoration projects.
