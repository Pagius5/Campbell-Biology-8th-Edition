Excellent choice. Organisms that have the ability to disperse long distances are often better colonizers. For plants, this is often done through seeds. For animals, flight can be important. Proximity to existing prairie also enhances colonization success. Movement corridors can also facilitate colonization.

Colonization of smaller sites is often limited by competition and predation more than at larger sites. In addition, colonization rates decrease over time due to increased competition for resources.
