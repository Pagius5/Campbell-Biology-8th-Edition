Metabolism produces wastes that must be eliminated from the body. This excretory function is the job of the renal system, most importantly the paired kidneys which filter the blood and selectively secrete and reabsorb substances to form urine. This case study allows you to explore some aspects of urine formation by manipulating the interstitial solute concentration and levels of two hormones, aldosterone and antidiuretic hormone (ADH).

Proceed by clicking on the Gradient Experiment button at the top of the screen.
