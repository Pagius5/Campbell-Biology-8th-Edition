The lung infection was caused by the species Pneumocystis carinii. It is a fungus, as are yeast and mushrooms.

Think about the relationship of the Pneumoniae carinii cells and HIV. How does HIV stimulate the growth of Pneumoniae carinii? This is an indirect effect. The fungal cells grow in an HIV-infected person not because the virus directly acts on the fungus, but because the virus kills the white blood cells whose job it is to kill the invading eukaryotic cells.

Drugs are available to slow the growth of these organisms. You will consider prescribing these drugs for your AIDS patients with this type of lung infection in the future.
