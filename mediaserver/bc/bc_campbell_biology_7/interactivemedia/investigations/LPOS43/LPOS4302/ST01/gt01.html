In this activity, you will investigate the spread of AIDS in the United States. You will propose a hypothesis that could explain why different regions of the country have different rates of HIV infection. You will then test the predictions of your hypothesis against data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

Biologists know that the virus called HIV causes AIDS. But how do they know that? Epidemiology, the study of the spread of disease through populations, suggests that an infectious agent causes AIDS. Epidemiologists initially realized that AIDS was particularly prevalent in homosexual men, intravenous drug users, and people with hemophilia. While these were the first groups in which AIDS was found to be particularly prevalent, today we know that AIDS can be spread to anyone by sexual contact.
