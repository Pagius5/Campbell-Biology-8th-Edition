To simulate the hypothesized conditions of early Earth, Miller's experiment included a large electrical discharge in the upper chamber, cooling just below the upper chamber, and heating of the lower chamber. When the molecules in the upper chamber are subjected to electricity, which mimics a lightning bolt, they rain down from the upper to the lower chamber. Note that this represents a short reaction time compared with the hundreds of millions of years in which life was forming.
