These are archegonia of maidenhair fern. The gametophyte is folded up on the slide giving a good side view of the gametangia. The archegonia are most obvious. The eggs are the brown balls embedded in the lower tissue of the gametophyte. Extending down from the egg you can see the neck and neck canal of each archegonium in focus. If you look carefully, you can see little green domes extending down between some of the archegonia. These are antheridia. Eggs that do not get fertilized abort and the archegonia turn dark brown. This is a good example of the principle that organisms produce more offspring than can survive to reproduce. It is also another excellent example of an organism where gametes are derived from cells that are the products of the mitosis of haploid parent cells.
