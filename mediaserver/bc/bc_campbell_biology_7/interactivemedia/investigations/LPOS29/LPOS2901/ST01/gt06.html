This is a germinating spore of a maidenhair fern. The brown spore wall has split and the haploid spore cell has undergone a few rounds of mitosis and cellular differentiation. The longer, thinner cell extending to the right is the first rhizoid. Note that it has very few plastids. The filament of thicker, shorter cells extending to the top of the image will develop into the photosynthetic portion of the gametophyte. Notice the numerous green plastids in its cells. This may be used as a good example of a case where a haploid product of meiosis begins to undergo mitotic divisions to develop into a haploid somatic state. Remember, not all products of meiosis are gametes.
