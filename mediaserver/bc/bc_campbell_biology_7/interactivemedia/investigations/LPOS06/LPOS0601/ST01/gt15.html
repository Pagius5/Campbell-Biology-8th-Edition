We are now at the scale of 100 µm, which is below the resolving power limit of the human eye (0.2 mm = 200 µm). The image shown at left is a mite, photographed with a scanning electron microscope.

Mites belong to the Arachnida, the class of Arthropods that includes scorpions and spiders. There are over 15,000 known species of mites, many of which are plant or animal parasites, and some of which carry disease. One species, the common dust mite, is a microscopic companion of humans, feeding on the dead skin cells that we shed, as well as furniture stuffing, wallpaper paste, and dust. It is also a common cause of allergies.
