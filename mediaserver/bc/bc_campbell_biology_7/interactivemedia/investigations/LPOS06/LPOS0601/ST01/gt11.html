We are now at the scale of 100 mm (10 cm, 0.1 m, about 4 inches).

This is the world of small animals, including squirrels, mice and other rodents, most birds, and sardines, as well as small flowers, mushrooms, and other organisms.

Warm-blooded animals face a unique problem as they get smaller. The ratio of surface area to volume gets larger, and they lose body heat more quickly. Small animals must eat more food in relation to body mass, in order to maintain body temperature. The Mediterranean shrew, the smallest mammal (about one-tenth the size of the squirrel shown at left) must eat 2-3 times its body mass each day!
