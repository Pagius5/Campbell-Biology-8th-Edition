This is the size range for typical prokaryotic cells. The image at left shows a cross-sectioned view of a typical bacterial cell as seen in a transmission electron microscope (TEM).

The micrometer unit, abbreviated µm (Greek mu + m for meter), is also commonly called the micron.

Note the following features:

-- No nucleus is present. This is the defining characteristic of a prokaryotic cell.
-- The cell is bounded by a cell wall that gives it rigid shape.
-- The cell's major inner compartment, the cytoplasm, contains many ribosomes, machines for protein synthesis.
-- DNA is localized in dense regions throughout the cytoplasm, called the nucleoid.
