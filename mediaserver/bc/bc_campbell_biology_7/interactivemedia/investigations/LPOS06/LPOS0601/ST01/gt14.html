This is the size range for typical eukaryotic cells, such as most of our body cells. The scanning electron micrograph to the left shows a human macrophage (gray) approaching a chain of Streptococcus pyogenes bacteria (yellow). Riding atop the macrophage is a spherical lymphocyte (pink). Both macrophages and lymphocytes can be found near an infection, and the interaction between these cells is important in eliminating infection.

Notice the size range of the cells in this image. Macrophages range from 15 to 25 µm, while many bacteria are 0.5-2 µm. These images have been colorized; electron microscope images have no inherent color.
