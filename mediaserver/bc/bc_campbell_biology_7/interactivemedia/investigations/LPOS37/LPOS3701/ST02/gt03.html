This behavior distinguishes Mg2+ deficiency from Fe3+ deficiency. Both minerals are needed to make green leaves; Mg2+ is part of the green chlorophyll molecule, and plants need Fe3+ to put Mg2+ into chlorophyll. But the two ions differ in mobility. Young leaves can take Mg2+, but not Fe3+, from old leaves. Thus, if the soil lacks Fe3+, the old leaves stay green while the young leaves grow yellow. If the soil lacks Mg2+, the old leaves turn yellow as they give up Mg2+ to make the young leaves green.
