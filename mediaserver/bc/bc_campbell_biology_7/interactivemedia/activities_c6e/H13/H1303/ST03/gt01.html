Now let's look at how crossing over creates even more genetic variability. During prophase I of meiosis, homologous chromosomes pair up very closely, and corresponding parts of two nonsister chromatids may trade places. This process of crossing over creates variation by producing chromosomes that combine the genes inherited from two parents. Here the process produced a total of four genetically different gametes.
