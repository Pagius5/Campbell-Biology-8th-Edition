There are 13 main islands and 14 smaller islands in the Galapagos Archipelago. Here we are approaching Isabella Island, a volcanic island and the largest of the islands. Isabella is on the western edge of the Galapagos Archipelago and is one of the newest islands in the group. This large island is made up of six volcanoes, five of which have been recently active. Iguanas, tortoises, and sea lions can be found on this island.
