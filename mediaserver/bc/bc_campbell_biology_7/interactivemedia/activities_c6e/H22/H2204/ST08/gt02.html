Climate

Tierra del Fuego is renowned for its inclement weather and nearly incessant westerly winds (from the west). The region lies in what is known as the roaring forties: "roaring" refers to the strength of the prevailing winds that develop due to the Antarctic land mass, and "forties" refers to the latitude (bounded on the north by 40 degrees south latitude). The annual precipitation in Punta Arenas is not representative of Tierra del Fuego. Most of the islands have very abundant rains—as in a temperate rainforest.
