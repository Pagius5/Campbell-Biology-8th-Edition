Background on Charles Darwin

At the age of 22, Charles Darwin finished his Bachelor of Arts degree at Cambridge University. He had not been a very diligent student, but he had done well enough to pass his exams. He had tried to follow his father into the field of medicine, but he abandoned that because he could not stand the sight of blood.

Darwin's plan was to become a pastor in a country church. He was not passionate about the ministry, but he thought it to be an acceptable profession that would fit in reasonably well with his love of being outdoors. His botany professor at Cambridge, John Henslow, was also a clergyman, and Henslow had encouraged Darwin's study of his real love: natural history.
