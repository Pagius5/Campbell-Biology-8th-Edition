What Darwin Did

Darwin stayed near Rio de Janeiro from April until July in 1832. For two weeks in April, he accompanied a local Englishman on a visit to his estate about one hundred miles inland. Darwin did little collecting during this excursion, but he made many observations about the flora and fauna.

When the excursion was over, Darwin rented a small cottage about 4 mi. from Rio at the base of Mount Corcovado,which rose directly from the sea to a height of 2300 ft. This cottage served as a home base and laboratory for extensive natural history collecting and study.

In the rich, verdant tropical forests of Brazil, Darwin collected and studied many vertebrates (such as monkeys, jaguarundi, tree frogs, humming birds, etc.). He also spent a great deal of time collecting invertebrates, especially flatworms, butterflies, beetles, and spiders. He was a meticulous, hard-working observer, and he sent many of his specimens back to Henslow in Cambridge.
