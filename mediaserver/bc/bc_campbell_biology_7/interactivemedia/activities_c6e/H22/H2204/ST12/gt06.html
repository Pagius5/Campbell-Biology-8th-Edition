Galapagos Research Today

The Galapagos Islands are still an important site for scientific research. Investigations range from avian biology, to geology, meteorology, oceanography and zoology. All research that is conducted on the Islands is done in conjunction with the Darwin Research Station (DRS) on Santa Cruz Island. The DRS conducts many of its own investigations as well as supervising the research done by foreigners.

Visit the Darwin Research Station on the World Wide Web. Explore the site to find out what research is currently being done.

As a service to researchers, the DRS posts Information for Visiting Scientists on its web pages, including the requirements for a project proposal, regulations for scientific work, information on visas, equipment, and supplies, and services that the DRS can provide.
