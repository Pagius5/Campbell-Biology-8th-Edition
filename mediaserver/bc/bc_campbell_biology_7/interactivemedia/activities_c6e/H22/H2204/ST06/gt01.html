About the Pampas

From July to October in 1832, the Beagle made port calls at Montevideo and Buenos Aires and then surveyed the coast south to Bahia Blanca. Much of this time for Darwin was spent working on collections. From April to December of 1833, the Beagle returned to again survey this area, and farther south along the coast of Argentina. The Pampas are fertile treeless plains in the eastern central region of Argentina. They are the richest grasslands in the world.
