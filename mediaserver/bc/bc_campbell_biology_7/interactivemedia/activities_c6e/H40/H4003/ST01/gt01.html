Connective tissue forms the framework of the body, binding and supporting other tissues. Connective tissue cells are often widely scattered, embedded in an extracellular matrix. The matrix is usually secreted by the cells and consists of fibers embedded in a foundation that may be liquid, jellylike, or solid. Some examples of connective tissues are bone, cartilage, and blood.
