Many events in the life cycle of a flowering plant (seed germination and flowering, for example) occur at different times of year. The environmental stimulus that most often triggers seasonal changes is called photoperiod, the relative lengths of night and day.

Some plants are called short-day (long-night) plants. For short-day plants, the critical night length is the minimum number of hours of darkness required for flowering. For long-day plants, the critical night length is the maximum number of hours of darkness required for flowering. Day-neutral plants flower when they mature, regardless of photoperiod.
