In endocytosis, membranes invaginate, or pinch in, to form a vesicle, moving the enclosed materials inside the cell. This process can take different forms, each involving its own specific cell machinery.

In phagocytosis, or "cell eating," the cell engulfs debris, bacteria, or other sizable objects. Phagocytosis occurs in specialized cells called phagocytes, which include macrophages, neutrophils, and other white blood cells. Invagination produces a vacuole, which usually fuses with one or more lysosomes containing hydrolytic enzymes. Materials in the vacuole are broken down by these enzymes and degraded.
