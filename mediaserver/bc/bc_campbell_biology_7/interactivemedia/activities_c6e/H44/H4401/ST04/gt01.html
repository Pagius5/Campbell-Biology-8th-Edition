A tiny branch of the renal artery carries blood to each nephron for processing. A tiny vein carries blood away. Blood pressure forces fluid through the walls of a cluster of capillaries called the glomerulus, and it collects in the cup-shaped Bowman's capsule. The filtrate is refined as it passes through the twisted nephron tubule. Useful substances are returned to the blood, and the remainder collects as urine. Refined urine is carried by the collecting duct into the renal pelvis for excretion from the body.
