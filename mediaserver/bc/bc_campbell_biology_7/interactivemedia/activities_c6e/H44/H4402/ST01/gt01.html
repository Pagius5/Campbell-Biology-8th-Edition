Nephrons regulate the composition of blood by a combination of three processes that transfer materials between the nephron tubules and the capillaries that serve them: filtration, secretion, and reabsorption. A nephron tubule and the blood vessels surrounding it have been straightened and simplified in this diagram. The gradation of color-- lighter on top, darker on the bottom-- corresponds to an increasing concentration of solutes in the interstitial fluid surrounding the nephron toward the center of the kidney.
