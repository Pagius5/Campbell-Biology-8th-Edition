As blood flows through the glomerulus-- a knot of porous capillaries-- water and virtually all other molecules small enough are forced by blood pressure out of the glomerulus and into Bowman's capsule. This process is called filtration, and the fluid that accumulates in Bowman's capsule is called filtrate.

Blood contains: blood cells, plasma proteins, water, sodium chloride, hydrogen ions, bicarbonate ions, urea, glucose, amino acids, and possibly some drugs and poisons.
