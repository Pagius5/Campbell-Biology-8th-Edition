A cross of an individual with a dominant phenotype to a homozygous recessive individual in order to find out its genotype is called a testcross. There are two possible outcomes of a testcross. As shown on the left, if all the F1 offspring have the dominant phenotype then the genotype of the parent with the dominant phenotype is homozygous dominant, OO in this example.

As shown on the right, if half of the F1offspring have the dominant phenotype and half have the recessive phenotype, then the genotype of the parent with the dominant phenotype is heterozygous, Oo in this example.
