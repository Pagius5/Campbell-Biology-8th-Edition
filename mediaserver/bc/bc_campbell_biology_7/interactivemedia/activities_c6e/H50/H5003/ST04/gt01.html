The diversity of organisms in aquatic biomes, as with terrestrial biomes, is similarly determined by biotic and abiotic factors. The major abiotic factors of aquatic biomes are sunlight penetration, availability of oxygen, nutrients available, salinity, and water temperature. Human activities can affect the biodiversity and stability of aquatic biomes.
