4. Describe an abiotic factor of marine biomes that is not significant in freshwater biomes.

Freshwater biomes lack the effects of a factor called salinity. Salinity describes the salt content of water. Marine environments generally have at least five times the salt content of fresh water. Many marine organisms can only withstand a narrow range of salinity. They will perish if the salt rises or lowers beyond this range. An estuary is an area with highly variable salinity and has an array of organisms adapted to the changing salt concentrations.
