Aquatic biomes make up a large part of the Earth's surface, covering more area than all terrestrial biomes added together. Aquatic biomes can be either freshwater or marine. Sunlight, temperature, oxygen content, and water depth are four of the most important abiotic factors that affect the make-up of the biotic communities that inhabit aquatic biomes. In marine environments, salt content, or salinity, is also a major factor. You will visit both freshwater and marine biomes in this activity.
