Freshwater biomes, as well as marine biomes, are usually divided into smaller parts called life zones. The types of organisms in a life zone are determined by the zone's abiotic and biotic factors. Abiotic factors making up a zone include temperature, the amount of oxygen dissolved in the water, and the amount of sunlight penetrating the water. The presence of algae or particular types of animals are important biotic factors that determine the distribution of other organisms in a freshwater life zone.
