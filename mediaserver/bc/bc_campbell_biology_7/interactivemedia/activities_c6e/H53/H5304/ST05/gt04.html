The point where the curves for immigration rate and extinction rate intersect is the equilibrium number of species. Below this point colonization exceeds extinction and the number of species will continue to increase. Above this point extinction exceeds colonization and the number of species will decline.

After a long time has passed since the emergence or disturbance of the island—hundreds to thousands of years—the number of species is predicted to be at this equilibrium number.
