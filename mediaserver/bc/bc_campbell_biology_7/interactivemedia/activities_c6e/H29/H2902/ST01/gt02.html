Correct. Cell walls are a feature common to cells of both aquatic algae and terrestrial plants, although they differ in chemical composition. Cell walls are needed to provide structural support at the cellular level. Algae living in fresh water have cytoplasm that is hypertonic to the water in which they are suspended. Thus, they need cell walls to limit the extent to which their cells can swell. Without cell walls the cells would swell until their plasma membranes burst, thus killing the cells.
