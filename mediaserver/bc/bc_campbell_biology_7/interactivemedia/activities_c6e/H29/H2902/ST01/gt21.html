That's correct. The water that is needed for terrestrial plant photosynthesis and for life itself is primarily available in the soil. But the soil is dark and photosynthesis is impossible there. Aerial shoots with leaves maximize the exposure of vascular plants to the energy available in sunlight. Carbon dioxide is also available in the air. Roots obtain water and mineral nutrients from the soil. With roots and shoots, terrestrial plants are able to obtain all of the resources they need to produce sugars by photosynthesis.
