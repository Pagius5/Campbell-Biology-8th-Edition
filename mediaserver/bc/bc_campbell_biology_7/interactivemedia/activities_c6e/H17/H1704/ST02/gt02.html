Messenger RNA (mRNA) carries the genetic message from the nucleus to the cytoplasm. Codons— triplets of RNA bases— spell out the amino acid sequence of a polypeptide, from a start codon near the 5' end of the mRNA to a stop codon near the 3' end. The portion of the mRNA that actually codes for a polypeptide is called the coding segment.
