This is a savanna in East Africa, which is an ecosystem that has grasses and tall, flat-topped trees called acacias. Here, we will study how energy flows through the ecosystem. Energy enters as sunlight, which is used by plants in photosynthesis. Plants are called producers because they produce the organic molecules that all living things need. Acacia trees are obviously producers, as are shrubs and grasses. Animals that feed on plants (herbivores) are primary consumers. An example of a primary consumer in Africa is the duiker. The name "duiker" means "diver," because when duikers are startled they run in a zigzag and then dive into the nearest undergrowth.
