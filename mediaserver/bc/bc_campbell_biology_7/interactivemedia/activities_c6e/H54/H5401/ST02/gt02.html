Notice how the cheetah's spine is so flexible that its rear legs hit the ground several feet in front of its front legs when it runs. Running obviously requires energy, and mammals typically use up about 95% of the energy from their food just in keeping warm, moving, and maintaining the chemical reactions in their body. For example, only 5% of the energy from an antelope, an herbivore, is passed on to the cheetah. This is similar to the "efficiency" of light bulbs-- roughly 95% of the energy a bulb uses is wasted as heat; only 5% is actually given off as light.
