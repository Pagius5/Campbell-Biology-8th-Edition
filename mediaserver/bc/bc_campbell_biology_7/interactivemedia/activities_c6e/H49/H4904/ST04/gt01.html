A nerve impulse stimulates the muscle cell. This triggers the sarcoplasmic reticulum to release stored calcium ions. Calcium ions interact with other proteins on the thin filament, opening up binding sites on the actin and making it possible for myosin heads to bind to the actin. As ADP and phosphate are released, the myosin head bends. This pulls on the thin filament, causing the thin filament to slide toward the center of the sarcomere. More ATP binds to the myosin head and the whole process repeats. The myosin head continues to move and pull on the actin filament and the muscle contracts as long as calcium ions are available to open up actin binding sites-- until the muscle fiber is fully contracted.
