A bacterium is placed on a growth medium. It replicates the plasmid at the same time as its own DNA. Its descendants form a clone of bacteria that all have copies of the recombinant plasmid. The ampicillin in the medium limits growth only to plasmid-containing cells, but which cells have plasmids with a human gene? Bacteria without human genes have intact lacZ genes; this allows them to break down a substance in the medium that stains their colonies blue. Thus, the bacteria with human genes are in the colorless colonies.
