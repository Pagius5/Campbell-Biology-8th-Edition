The location of genes is designated by an established labeling system for chromosomes. Shown here is a drawing of human chromosome 17 showing its characteristic major banding pattern as it appears after staining. By convention, chromosomes are drawn with the short arm on top labeled p (for petit) and the long arm on the bottom labeled q (for queue). To indicate the location of a gene on a particular chromosome, the chromosome number is followed by p or q and then a number that represents the band and sub-band. For example 17p13.3 designates the p arm of chromosome 17, band 13, sub-band 3. In some cases only an approximate location is known so a range of numbers is given.
