Understanding the structure and life cycle of HIV, the AIDS virus, is critically important to dealing with the disease. HIV is covered by an envelope derived from a host cell membrane. Glycoproteins studding the envelope recognize and bind to receptor molecules on the host cell. A protein coat surrounds the viral genetic material, which consists of two molecules of single-stranded RNA. The enzyme reverse transcriptase enables the virus to make DNA from an RNA template, a trick for which this group of viruses is named-- retroviruses.
