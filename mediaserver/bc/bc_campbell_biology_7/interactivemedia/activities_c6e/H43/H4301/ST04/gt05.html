When the helper T cell receptor binds with the B cell class II MHC-antigen complex, it begins secreting interleukin-2 and other cytokines that activate the B cells. Activated B cells divide repeatedly into memory B cells and plasma cells. The plasma cells produce and secrete large numbers of antibodies that are specific to the original antigen. In this humoral response to a T-dependent antigen, both the helper T cells and B cells are identified and selected for activity by the antigen.
