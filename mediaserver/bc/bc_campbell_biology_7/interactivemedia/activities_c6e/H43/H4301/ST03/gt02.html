The B cell that successfully binds to the antigen is stimulated to proliferate into a clone of cells with the same specificity. This process of antigen-selected proliferation is known as clonal selection. B cell proliferation produces clones of plasma cells and memory cells. Memory cells are long-lived cells that can respond rapidly upon subsequent exposure to the same antigen. They account for the rapidity of the secondary immune response and long-term immunity to a specific disease.
