Correct. The helper T (TH) cell interacts with the antigen-bearing macrophage through its T cell receptor and the cell surface protein CD4. Each helper T cell receptor is unique, meaning that only a few helper T cell receptors can bind to the specific antigen-class II MHC combination. The helper T cell is stimulated by this contact, as well as by the cytokine interleukin-1. Click on the cell in the picture that produces and secretes interleukin-1.
