The body's immune responses can be divided into the humoral (or antibody-mediated) response and the cell-mediated response. In both types of response, the helper T cell plays a central coordinating role.

Microbes like bacteria or viruses carry antigen molecules on their surfaces that identify them as foreign. Choosing from the cells shown at the bottom of the screen, click on the phagocytic leukocyte that can recognize these antigens as foreign and engulf the microbe by phagocytosis.
