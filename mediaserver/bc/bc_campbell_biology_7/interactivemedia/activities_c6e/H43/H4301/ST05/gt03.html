Agglutination is the process whereby bacteria or viruses are effectively neutralized by clumping. Antibodies have at least two binding sites, allowing them to crosslink microbes by binding to identical antigens on two different cells.

Microbes that are tagged by antibodies are then eliminated by phagocytosis. In this process, called opsonization, the bound antibodies enhance macrophage attachment to the microbes, allowing them to be easily engulfed and destroyed by phagocytosis.
