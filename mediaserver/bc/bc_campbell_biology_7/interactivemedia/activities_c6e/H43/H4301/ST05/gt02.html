Neutralization of bacteria or viruses occurs when antibodies bind to and block the activity of the antigen. Neutralized viruses cannot invade host cells.

Microbes and soluble antigens that are coated with antibodies are then eliminated by phagocytosis. In this process, called opsonization, the bound antibodies enhance macrophage attachment to the microbes, allowing them to be easily engulfed and destroyed by phagocytosis.
