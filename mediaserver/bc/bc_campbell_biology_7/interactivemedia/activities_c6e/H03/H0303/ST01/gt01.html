Occasionally, a hydrogen atom involved in a hydrogen bond between two water molecules shifts from one molecule to another. The hydrogen atom leaves its electron behind, so what actually shifts is a hydrogen ion, a single proton with a positive charge. The water molecule that lost the hydrogen ion is now a hydroxide ion, OH-, which has an extra electron and a negative charge. The hydrogen ion binds to the attracting water molecule, forming a hydronium ion, H3O+.
