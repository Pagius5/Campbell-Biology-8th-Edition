Note that the more basic the solution, the higher the pH. As the base removes hydrogen ions from solution, the concentration of hydroxide ions (OH-) exceeds the concentration of hydrogen ions. Remember that our animation is meant only to suggest the changes in concentration. The actual proportions of H+ and OH- ions are very small. Note also that each pH unit represents a tenfold change in H+ concentration. So a milliliter of household bleach at pH 12 contains one-tenth the hydrogen ions in a milliliter of household ammonia at pH 11. The pHs of other solutions are shown next to the scale.
