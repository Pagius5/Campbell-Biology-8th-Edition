Note that the more acidic the solution, the lower the pH. As the solution becomes more and more acidic, the concentration of hydrogen ions (H+) exceeds the concentration of hydroxide ions. Remember that our animation is meant only to suggest the changes in concentration. The actual proportions of H+ and OH- ions are very small. Note also that each pH unit represents a tenfold change in H+ concentration. So a milliliter of gastric juice at pH 2 contains ten times as many hydrogen ions as a milliliter of vinegar at pH 3.
