Correct. Starting at RNA primers, DNA polymerase joins DNA nucleotides to form new DNA strands along the old ones. Note that DNA polymerase can only move along a template strand in the 3' to 5' direction, making new DNA 5' to 3'. This means that along one template strand, DNA polymerase can work toward the widening replication fork, but along the other strand it must work in the opposite direction, making the new DNA in short segments.
