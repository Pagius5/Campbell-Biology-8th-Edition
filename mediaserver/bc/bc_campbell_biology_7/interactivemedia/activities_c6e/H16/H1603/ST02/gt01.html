The yellow and red phosphate groups make the two sugar-phosphate backbones easy to spot. They're on the outside of the double helix. We will highlight the backbones in blue.

Now let's look at the crossbars connecting the backbones. Each crossbar is a hydrogen-bonded pair of bases, one from each DNA strand. An adenine is always paired with a thymine, and a guanine with a cytosine.
