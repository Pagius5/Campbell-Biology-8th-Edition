Hormones act as chemical signals, but how do they trigger changes in particular target cells? Nonsteroid hormones— hormones derived from amino acids (oxytocin and epinephrine, for example)— work via signal transduction pathways to activate or inhibit proteins within cells. These hormones can trigger an immediate response in target cells by turning cell components on or off. Several hormones are shown outside the cell.
