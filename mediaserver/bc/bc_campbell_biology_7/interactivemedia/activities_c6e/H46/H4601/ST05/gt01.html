The hypothalamus produces a releasing hormone that regulates the secretion of follicle stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH) from the anterior pituitary. FSH stimulates sperm production by the testes. LH stimulates the testes to secrete androgens—male sex hormones. Testosterone is the most important androgen secreted by the testes. Androgens shape the development of the male reproductive system. They stimulate sperm production and trigger development of secondary sex characteristics such as beard growth.
