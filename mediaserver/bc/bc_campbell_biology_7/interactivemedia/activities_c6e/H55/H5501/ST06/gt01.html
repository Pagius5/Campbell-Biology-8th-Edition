The human population of Madagascar is growing rapidly. The average woman has more than 5 children. The population doubles about every 25 years. The graph shows the population growth from 1950 to 2000. Move the line for the year 2050 to where you think it should be, then click on "Done" to show the real estimate.

This rapidly growing population puts great pressure on natural ecosystems. Madagascar is a very poor country, with per capita income of only $730 a year (compared to $30,500 per capita in the US). People in rural areas are forced to cut more forest to grow crops to feed their families.
