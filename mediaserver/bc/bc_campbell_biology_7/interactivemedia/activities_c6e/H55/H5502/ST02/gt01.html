Fire ants can spread naturally up to 10 miles per year when winged adults fly away and start a new colony. An established colony can produce up to 5,000 new queens a year, each of which can start a new colony.

Colonies can also be spread accidentally by humans, either in ships (for example, in soil used as ballast), or in trucks and trains that transport soil or plants.
