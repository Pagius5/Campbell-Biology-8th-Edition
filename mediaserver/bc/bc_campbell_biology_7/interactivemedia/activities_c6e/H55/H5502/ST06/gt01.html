One new technique that is being investigated is to introduce natural enemies of the fire ants.

Two new species have been introduced in the U.S. to try to control fire ants. The first is a protist called Thelohania solenopsae. This infects the queen and the eggs that she lays, and gradually kills the colony. This species was introduced in 1998.

The second species is a phorid fly called Pseudacteon tricuspus. These flies inject their eggs into the fire ants. The egg hatches into a larva that eats its way into the ant's head. Eventually, the ant's head falls off, so these flies are often called "ant-decapitating flies". Not surprisingly, this kills the ant, and the larva emerges from the ant's head. This fly was first released in the U.S. in 1997.
