Electronegativity is the tendency for an atom to pull electrons toward itself. Two atoms of the same element have equal electronegativities; in a covalent bond they share electrons equally, forming a nonpolar covalent bond. The bond between the atoms in a molecule of hydrogen gas is a nonpolar covalent bond. Carbon and hydrogen have similar electronegativities. In methane, they share their electrons more-or-less equally. The carbon-hydrogen covalent bond is also relatively nonpolar.
