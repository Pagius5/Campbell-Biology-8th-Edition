Different types of models are used to represent individual color-coded atoms and their three-dimensional geometry. We can build models of atoms and molecules in several ways. In the space-filling model, the shape of each atom is depicted as a sphere, whose color depends on the particular element. In the ball-and-stick model, small colored balls represent atoms, and dowel-shaped sticks represent the covalent bonds that join them. Shown here are space-filling and ball-and-stick models of the sugar glucose, whose structural formula is C6H12O6. Notice that carbon atoms are depicted as light gray spheres, oxygen atoms as red spheres, and hydrogen atoms as white spheres. To see the atoms labeled, click on the buttons above the molecules.
