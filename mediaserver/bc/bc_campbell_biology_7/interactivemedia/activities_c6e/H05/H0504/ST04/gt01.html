Phospholipids are important components of cell membranes. Like fats, phospholipid molecules contain fatty acid tails linked to a glycerol portion, but in phospholipids a group containing phosphate replaces one of the tails. This makes the molecule ambivalent with regard to water: Part of it is polar and mixes with water, while part of it is nonpolar and excluded from water.
