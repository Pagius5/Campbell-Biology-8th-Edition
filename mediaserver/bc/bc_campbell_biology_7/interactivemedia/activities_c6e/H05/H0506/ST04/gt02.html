The irregular folding of tertiary structure results from interactions among the R groups of amino acids. Acidic and basic R groups ionize, and these positively and negatively charged groups may form ionic bonds. Polar forces also contribute to tertiary structure. Hydrophilic (polar) R groups may hydrogen bond with one another, or turn outward and hydrogen bond with the surrounding water. Hydrophobic (nonpolar) R groups cluster on the inside of the protein, away from water. Tertiary structure may be further stabilized by strong covalent bonds between sulfur atoms in certain R groups.
