This illustration shows some of the amino acids making up the primary structure of a protein. The structure of a single generalized amino acid is shown below. The main backbone of every amino acid is the same. This is what forms the backbone of the polypeptide chain. It is the R-group (which projects out from the backbone) that makes each of the twenty kinds of amino acids unique. Different amino acids have different properties that affect the folding of a protein. Thus, primary structure ultimately determines the shape of a protein, which determines its function.
