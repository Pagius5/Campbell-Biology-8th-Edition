In most proteins, parts of the polypeptide chain are coiled or folded, forming twists and corrugations. This is secondary structure. The turns and folds of secondary structure contribute to the protein's overall shape. One kind of secondary structure is the alpha helix, where the chain twists. Another is the pleated sheet, where the chain folds back on itself or where two regions of the chain lie parallel to one another.
