The overall growth of the human population has been increasing rapidly over the past 500 years. However, population growth is not the same from one country to another. Government leaders are very interested to know future trends in their nations' populations and its age structures to help anticipate upcoming needs and use of resources. This explains the importance that many countries place on conducting an accurate census of the population.

In this activity, you will investigate age-structure diagrams that help governments and global agencies determine future population trends and needs.

The age-structure diagram that you see is characteristic of developing nations. Click on the questions to interpret the meaning and investigate the applications of the diagram.
