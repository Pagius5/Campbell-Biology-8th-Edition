The decline in this period is the result of a massive deadly epidemic in Europe:

Infectious disease is a natural population control factor for most animals and plants. Congested living conditions in urban areas led to the rapid spread of a disease called the bubonic plague or Black Death. Asian merchants introduced into Europe the bacterium that causes bubonic plague. Urban rats harbored the disease, ensuring its persistence and aiding its spread. Within a few years of its introduction the disease had killed over one-third of the entire European population.
