The populations of animals, plants, and other organisms in an ecosystem are determined by many environmental factors. Populations tend to reach a certain maximum level called the carrying capacity.

Humans are in many ways subject to the same factors that affect the populations of other animals. But, unlike other animals, humans have been able to modify the environment and exploit new environments to maintain almost unlimited population growth.
