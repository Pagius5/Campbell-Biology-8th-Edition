The acidic contents of the stomach now enter the duodenum, the first portion of the small intestine. The duodenum secretes several hormones called enterogastrones that control digestion.

Acid in the stomach contents triggers duodenum to secrete an enterogastrone that stimulates the pancreas to release bicarbonate, a base that neutralizes the acid.
