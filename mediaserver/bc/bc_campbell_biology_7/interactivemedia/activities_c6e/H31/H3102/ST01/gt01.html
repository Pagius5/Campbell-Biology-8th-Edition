The life cycles of many fungi include both sexual and asexual reproduction.

Fungal spores are haploid. In a suitable environment, a spore germinates, dividing by mitosis to produce a tangle of fibers called hyphae, which collectively form a mass called a mycelium.

Asexual reproduction is simple: Mitosis produces spores, which then germinate and develop into hyphae.

Sexual reproduction is more complicated: First hyphae fuse, in a process called plasmogamy. Nuclei pair up, but do not fuse. A heterokaryotic mycelium develops. Eventually, nuclei fuse, producing a diploid stage (zygote). This fusion is called karyogamy. Diploid cells (zygotes) then undergo meiosis, producing haploid spores.
