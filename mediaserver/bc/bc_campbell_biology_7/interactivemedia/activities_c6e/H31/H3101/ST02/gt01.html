The spores that land on a moist substrate grow into long branching hyphae. A tangled network of hyphae is called a mycelium. Mycelia can be huge. There are reports that the species Armillaria bulbosa have spreading mycelia that can cover hundreds of acres. As a group, fungi are decomposers and play a key role in the food web. As mycelia grow, they release digestive enzymes into their surroundings. These enzymes break down the macromolecules found in leaves and other decaying matter, liberating smaller subunits like sugars and amino acids.
