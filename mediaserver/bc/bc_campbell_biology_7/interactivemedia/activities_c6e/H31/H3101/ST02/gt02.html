To begin their extracellular digestion, fungi must first release many digestive enzymes. These enzymes decompose complex molecules like cellulose, RNA, proteins, and fats into simpler subunits like glucose, nucleic acids, amino acids, and fatty acids. Once these smaller monomers are produced, the fungal cell absorbs them into its cytoplasm where they are used to provide energy for the fungal cell or as building blocks for fungal macromolecules.
