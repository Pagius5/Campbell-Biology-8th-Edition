Is a grasshopper a protostome or a deuterostome? Recall that a protostome has spiral and determinate cleavage, the coelom is formed by the splitting of solid masses of mesoderm, and the mouth develops from the blastopore. In contrast, a deuterostome has radial and indeterminate cleavage, the coelom is formed from folds of the archenteron, and the anus develops from the blastopore.
