The oxidation of glucose continues in the citric acid cycle. Pyruvate molecules formed during glycolysis diffuse from the cytosol into the mitochondrion, but pyruvate itself does not enter the citric acid cycle. A reaction occurs that removes a carbon atom, releasing it in carbon dioxide. Electrons are transferred to an NADH molecule, storing energy. Coenzyme A, or CoA, joins with the 2-carbon fragment, forming acetyl CoA.
