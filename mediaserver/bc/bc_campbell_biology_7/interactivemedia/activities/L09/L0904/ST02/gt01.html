One molecule of acetyl CoA enters the citric acid cycle. The 2-carbon fragment of acetyl CoA attaches to the 4-carbon molecule oxaloacetate in the first reaction of the cycle. This forms citrate. In a series of steps, bonds break and reform. Two carbon atoms are released, one at a time, in molecules of carbon dioxide. Electrons are carried off by molecules of NADH and FADH2. One step produces an ATP molecule by substrate-level phosphorylation. A 4-carbon oxaloacetate molecule is regenerated.
