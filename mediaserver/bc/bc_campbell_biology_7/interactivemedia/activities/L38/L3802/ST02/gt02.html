Starting from a pollen grain at the stigma (which is not shown), a pollen tube grows down through the carpel to the ovule. It carries two sperm that enter the ovule. One sperm fuses with the egg cell to make a zygote, which is the first cell of the embryo. The other sperm fuses with two nuclei in the central cell, making a triploid nucleus. This triploid cell will grow and divide to make a food-storing tissue, the endosperm.
