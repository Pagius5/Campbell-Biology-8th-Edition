As you just saw, some parts of a tree undergo primary growth at the same time that other parts undergo secondary growth. If the roots were visible, you would see the same thing in the roots.

Primary growth occurs in young leaves and elongating parts of stems and roots. Apical meristems (regions of rapid cell division) provide cells for primary growth. Secondary growth occurs in regions of stems or roots that have completed their elongation. Leaves never have secondary growth.

Secondary growth doesn't occur in many short-lived eudicots and most monocots, such as palms and grasses.
