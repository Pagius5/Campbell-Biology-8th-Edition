After fertilization, three processes establish an animal's basic body plan: cleavage, gastrulation, and organogenesis. Processes taking several hours have been speeded up in this time-lapse video. Cleavage divides the zygote into a ball of many smaller cells. The first division splits the zygote in two. Each of these cells divides, forming four cells. Each cell in the early embryo is called a blastomere. Division continues, forming a multicellular ball called a morula. Over several hours, cleavage eventually creates a hollow ball of cells called a blastula. The cavity inside the blastula is called the blastocoel.
