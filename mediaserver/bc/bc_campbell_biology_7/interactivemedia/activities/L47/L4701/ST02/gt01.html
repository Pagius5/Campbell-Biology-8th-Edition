Sperm surround sea urchin eggs and lock onto egg membranes. Fusion of the membranes of a sperm and an egg triggers a cortical reaction: The vitelline layer quickly swells away from the egg membrane and creates a fertilization membrane that blocks entry of additional sperm. Fertilization combines the haploid sets of chromosomes of sperm and egg into a diploid zygote. Membrane fusion also activates the egg, triggering subsequent embryonic development.
