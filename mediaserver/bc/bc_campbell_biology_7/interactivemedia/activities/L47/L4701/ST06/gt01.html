Gastrulation is complete when the embryo consists of three tissue layers—ectoderm, endoderm, and mesoderm. The ectoderm is the outer layer of the embryo; during the subsequent process of organogenesis in vertebrates, it develops into the outer layer of skin and the nervous system. Endoderm lines the rudimentary digestive tract of the gastrula; in vertebrates, it eventually forms the lining of the stomach and intestine, and digestive glands such as the liver and pancreas. In between these two layers lies the mesoderm, which started with the mesenchyme cells and develops into most remaining organs and tissues, such as the skeleton, muscles, and circulatory system.
