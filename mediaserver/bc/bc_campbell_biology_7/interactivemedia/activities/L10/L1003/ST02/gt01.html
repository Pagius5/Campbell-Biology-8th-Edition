The thylakoid membrane in chloroplasts contains light-absorbing molecules called pigments. Different pigments absorb light of different wavelengths. One pigment, chlorophyll a, absorbs mainly blue-violet and red light. This molecule participates directly in the light reactions. Another molecule, chlorophyll b, absorbs mainly blue and orange light. We do not see these absorbed colors when we look at a leaf. Instead, we see the green wavelengths that are reflected back from the leaf or transmitted through it.
