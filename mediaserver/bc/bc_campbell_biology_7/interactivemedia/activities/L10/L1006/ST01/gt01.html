In warm, dry conditions many of the adaptations that plants make to conserve water reduce the availability of carbon dioxide to the chloroplasts. In C3 plants this increases photorespiration and greatly lowers the efficiency of photosynthesis.

C4 photosynthesis and CAM photosynthesis are adaptations that capture carbon dioxide and then transport it to the chloroplasts or store it for later use – eliminating photorespiration.
