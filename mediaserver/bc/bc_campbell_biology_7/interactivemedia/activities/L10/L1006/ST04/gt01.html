While both C4 and CAM plants fix carbon dioxide into four–carbon compounds, C4 plants have important anatomical adaptations not found in the CAM plants.

In C3 plants and CAM plants, chloroplasts are scattered throughout the mesophyll with large numbers found in the long uppermost cells called palisade cells.

In C4 plants, the chloroplasts are concentrated in the vascular bundle sheath cells. The four–carbon molecules produced in the mesophyll cells are transported to the chloroplasts in these cells, where they are broken down to release carbon dioxide.

Actively transporting carbon dioxide into these chloroplasts raises the concentration of carbon dioxide inside the chloroplasts and eliminates losses to photorespiration.

This process also speeds the entrance of carbon dioxide into the leaf, allowing the stomata to open less and reducing water loss.
