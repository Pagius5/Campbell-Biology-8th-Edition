The four–carbon molecules enter chloroplasts where the carbon dioxide will be released to be used in the Calvin cycle.

Both the light reactions and the Calvin cycle are the same in all types of photosynthesis. The enzyme Rubisco fixes carbon dioxide within the chloroplast.

As opposed to C3photosynthesis, C4 photosynthesis and CAM photosynthesis first fix carbon dioxide into four–carbon compounds in the cytoplasm of the mesophyll cells.

Lowering the concentration of carbon dioxide inside the leaf by fixing it into four–carbon molecules creates a steeper concentration gradient, which increases the diffusion of carbon dioxide into the plant from the atmosphere. This permits C4 plants to have smaller openings at their guard cells, thus reducing water loss.
