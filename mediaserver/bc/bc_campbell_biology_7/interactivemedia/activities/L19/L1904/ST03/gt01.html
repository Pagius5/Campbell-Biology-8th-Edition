The life span of mRNA molecules helps determine how much of a protein is made. Nucleotide sequences that affect mRNA longevity are often found in the 3' untranslated region following the stop codon. Enzymes attack the poly-A tail first, and eventually the rest of the mRNA. Depending on its ability to resist this degradation, a eukaryotic mRNA molecule may last from hours to weeks. The longer an mRNA lasts, the more protein is made.
