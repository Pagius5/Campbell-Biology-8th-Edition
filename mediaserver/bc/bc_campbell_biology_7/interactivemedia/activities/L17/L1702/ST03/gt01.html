RNA polymerase moves along the DNA, pairing up RNA nucleotides with their DNA complements— adding nucleotides to the end of the growing RNA molecule.

Here's a close-up view of elongation. Only one DNA strand— the template strand— serves as a template for RNA synthesis. RNA polymerase moves along the DNA strand in the 3' to 5' direction, adding nucleotides to the 3' end of the RNA chain. Note that U in RNA pairs with A in DNA.
