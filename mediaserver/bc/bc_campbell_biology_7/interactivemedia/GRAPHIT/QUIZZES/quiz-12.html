 There has been a catastrophic increase in atmospheric CO2 concentrations over the past 50 years.

 This data set is meaningless because CO2 concentrations and surface temperatures are constantly fluctuating over geologic time scales.
 This data is consistent with the hypothesis that increased atmospheric concentrations of CO2 may be causing an increase in average surface temperatures.
