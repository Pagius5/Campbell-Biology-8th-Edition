Dissolved Oxygen
Additional Help for Sample Problem
  1. Put the bottles in order from least to most oxygen. The 0% bottle will be the one that has the least oxygen. The 100% light bottle will have the most oxygen. If you do this correctly, bottle C got 10% light.

  2. Calculate gross productivity. Remember that the bottle with 0% light is the dark bottle. It has 1 mg O2/1. The light bottle is the bottle you are trying to figure productivity for. To calculate gross productivity, you subtract the % O2 in the dark bottle from the % O2 in the light bottle. Let's do the 10% light bottle as an example.

    Gross productivity = 3 mg O2/1 - 1 mg O2/1 = 2 mg O2/1

  3. Calculate net productivity. Again, the light bottle is the one you are doing the calculation for, and you are given the value for the initial bottle as 4 mg O2/1. To calculate net productivity you subtract the % O2 in the initial bottle from the % O2 in the light bottle. Let's do this for the 10% light bottle again:

    Net productivity = 3 mg O2/1 - 4 mg O2/1 = -1 mg O2/1
