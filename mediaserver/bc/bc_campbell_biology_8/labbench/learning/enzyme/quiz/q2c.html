Correct! The rate of the reaction drops when the enzyme no longer has a maximum number of substrate molecules to interact with. Above the maximum substrate concentration, the rate will not be increased by adding more substrate; the enzyme is already working as fast as it can. An enzyme can catalyze a certain number of reactions per second, and if there is not sufficient substrate present for it to work at its maximum velocity, the rate will decrease. Therefore, to keep the enzyme working at its maximum, you must add more substrate.
